OOP LABORATORY

JULIE ANN ROSE VILLAHERMOSA

In this activity laboratory, we will be exploring access modifiers in programming. Access modifiers determine the visibility and accessibility of classes, methods, and variables within a program. By creating our own access modifiers, we can understand how they impact the encapsulation and security of our code. ........

We can experiment with different access levels such as public, private, protected, and default, and observe how they restrict or allow access to different parts of our program. This hands-on exercise will enhance our understanding of access control in object-oriented programming.

OOP LABORATORY

GILBERT NAVARRO

In this activity laboratory, we will explore the concept of inheritance in object-oriented programming. Inheritance allows for the creation of new classes based on existing classes, called parent or base classes. ........

By creating our own examples of inheritance, we can demonstrate how derived or child classes inherit attributes and behaviors from their parent classes, promoting code reuse and modularity. We can experiment with different levels of inheritance, such as single, multiple, and hierarchical inheritance, to understand how they shape the relationships and hierarchy between classes.
